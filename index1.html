What is climate change?

Climate change is the long-term alteration of the average weather patterns that define Earth's local, regional and global climates. These changes have a broad range of observed effects that are synonymous with the term. Climate change is caused by factors such as biotic processes, variations in solar radiation received by Earth, plate tectonics, and volcanic eruptions. Certain human activities have been identified as primary causes of ongoing climate change, often referred to as global warming.

What are the causes of climate change?

Climate change is largely caused by human activities that increase the concentration of greenhouse gases (GHGs) in the atmosphere. These gases trap heat and warm the planet. The main sources of GHGs are:

  • Burning fossil fuels (coal, oil and gas) for energy, transportation and industry.
  • Deforestation and land use change that reduce the capacity of plants to absorb carbon dioxide.
  • Agriculture and livestock production that emit methane and nitrous oxide.
  • Waste management and industrial processes that release various GHGs.

What are the effects of climate change?

Climate change has significant impacts on the environment, society and economy, such as[^4^][4]:

  • Rising global temperatures and sea levels, melting ice caps and glaciers, and changing precipitation patterns.
  • Increasing the frequency and intensity of extreme weather events, such as heat waves, droughts, floods, storms and wildfires.
  • Threatening the survival and distribution of plants and animals, disrupting the ecosystems and biodiversity.
  • Affecting the availability and quality of water, food and health, especially for the vulnerable and poor populations.
  • Reducing the productivity and profitability of various sectors, such as agriculture, tourism, fisheries and energy.

How can we prevent climate change?

Climate change can be prevented or mitigated by reducing the emissions of GHGs and enhancing the sinks of carbon. Some of the actions that can be taken are:

  • Switching to renewable and clean sources of energy, such as solar, wind, hydro and biomass.
  • Improving the energy efficiency and conservation of buildings, appliances, vehicles and industries.
  • Promoting the use of public transportation, cycling, walking and electric vehicles.
  • Restoring and protecting the forests, wetlands, grasslands and oceans that store carbon and provide other ecosystem services.
  • Adopting sustainable practices in agriculture, such as organic farming, agroforestry, crop rotation and integrated pest management.
  • Reducing the consumption and waste of food, water, materials and products, and recycling and reusing them.
  • Educating and empowering the public, businesses and governments to take action and support the policies and initiatives that address climate change.
